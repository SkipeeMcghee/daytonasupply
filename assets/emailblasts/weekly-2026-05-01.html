This week's packing note
One bad box can ruin a good shipment.
Packing is not just putting something in a box and hoping for the best. The right materials help prevent damage, returns, frustration, and the kind of wasted money that starts with a split seam and ends with an apology.
Three quick ways to dodge shipping damage
1
Start with a fresh box
Use a fresh, sturdy box for anything heavy, fragile, valuable, or oddly shaped. Reused boxes do not always warn you before they fail.
2
Stop the item from shifting
Cushion the item so it cannot move around inside the box. If it can slide, bounce, or rattle, it can probably break.
3
Tape like you mean it
Use quality packing tape and seal all major seams. Cheap tape has a real talent for giving up halfway to the destination.
